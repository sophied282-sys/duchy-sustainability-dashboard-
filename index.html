Nature & emissions progress
Net zero 2032 — critical gap
From the 2023/24 reported position, the pathway to the 2032 net zero target requires material reductions across the remaining footprint. The challenge is almost entirely Scope 3 — particularly tenant farm engagement and land management transition.
Carbon removals growing ↑
Woodland sequestration up 4.4% to 28,246 tCO₂e. Scaling this is the most controllable lever available to the Duchy.
Natural capital: 30% by 2030
Target to improve natural capital by 30% by 2030 and 50% by 2050. Data collection across 52,449 hectares is the immediate priority.

Why this matters
Operational emissions rose in 2023/24
Scope 1 & 2 emissions remain very small relative to Scope 3, but the increase from baseline is still useful as a management-control indicator for estate buildings, fuels and electricity use. The gas boiler in the London office was replaced during 2023/24 and this year contributed to 15% reduction in GHGs from the London office heating and hot water (taken from 2025 Annual Report).
